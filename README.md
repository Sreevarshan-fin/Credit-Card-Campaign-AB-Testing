

# Credit Card Campaign A/B Testing & Segmentation Analysis

## Project Overview

This project evaluates a targeted credit card campaign for young customers (ages 18–25) using customer, credit score, and transaction data. The analysis combines exploratory data analysis, customer segmentation, power analysis, and A/B hypothesis testing to validate campaign effectiveness with statistical evidence. A controlled experiment was designed with proper sample size planning, and campaign performance was measured using Z-test and effect size metrics.

---

## Problem Statement

The bank planned to launch a new credit card campaign for young adults but faced uncertainty around customer response, segment suitability, and credit risk. Launching without validation could result in low adoption and poor return on marketing spend. The challenge was to identify the right segment and verify campaign effectiveness through a statistically valid A/B testing framework instead of assumption-based targeting.

---

## Objective

* Analyze customer spending and credit behavior
* Identify opportunity gaps in the 18–25 segment
* Segment customers by risk and behavior
* Design a statistically powered A/B experiment
* Calculate required sample size using power analysis
* Test campaign uplift using hypothesis testing
* Measure both statistical and practical impact

---

## Results

* A/B test executed with controlled random group assignment
* Test group conversion ≈ **40%**
* Test group average spend higher than control group
* Z-test showed **statistically significant improvement (p < 0.05)**
* Observed Effect Size (Cohen’s d) ≈ **0.49** → medium practical impact
* Young segment showed lower baseline usage but strong campaign responsiveness

---

## Business Impact

* Enabled data-driven campaign rollout decision
* Reduced launch risk through statistical validation
* Improved young-segment targeting strategy
* Supported evidence-based offer design
* Demonstrated experiment-driven marketing approach instead of guesswork
